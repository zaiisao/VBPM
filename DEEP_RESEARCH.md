## Research question

How do VAE researchers prevent latent collapse and bridge the train-vs-inference gap, and when do structured generative priors beat discriminative models — applied to CHART, a sequential variational *bar-pointer* state-space VAE for beat/downbeat tracking. The four failure modes investigated:

1. **Posterior / KL collapse** — under β=1 the KL → ~0 (kl_phase→0.003), latents go dead, free-run F→0.
2. **Train→inference free-running gap (exposure bias)** — trained teacher-forced on posterior samples, deployed free-running an audio-blind prior; phase drifts and tempo random-walks.
3. **Structured prior vs discriminative baseline** — the bar-pointer tracker rides ~0.10 below peak-picking the frontend on clean data; the prior seems to help only at low SNR.
4. **Discrete-latent collapse** — the Categorical meter/bar latent collapses (KL≈0), downbeats=0.000.

---

## Executive summary

VAE researchers prevent latent collapse with a layered toolkit: KL annealing/warm-up (Bowman 2016) as basic hygiene, decoder weakening (word dropout / latent-only decoders), minimum-rate constraints (free-bits, delta-VAE) that guarantee non-zero rate without weakening the decoder, and training-dynamics fixes (lagging/aggressive inference-network optimization, He 2019) — grounded in theory showing collapse stems from inference-network lag and likelihood local maxima rather than the ELBO itself (Lucas 2019). For sequential/dynamical VAEs (the DVAE family CHART belongs to: VRNN, SRNN, STORN, DVBF, Kalman-VAE per Girin 2021), the decisive design choice is an observation-history-conditioned prior (VRNN) and dynamics-enforcing inference (DVBF), plus auxiliary backward-prediction costs (Z-Forcing) that keep latents alive and beat KL annealing. The train-vs-inference free-running gap is the well-known exposure-bias problem, which afflicts both teacher forcing and scheduled sampling. On generative-vs-discriminative, Ng & Jordan's foundational result predicts CHART's exact symptom: structured/generative models win in low-data/low-SNR regimes (converging in O(log n) samples) but discriminative models overtake asymptotically (lower error with O(n) samples) — implying CHART's bar-pointer prior is a regularizer whose value lies at low SNR, uncertainty, and joint structure rather than high-SNR accuracy.

## Findings

### 1. Posterior/KL collapse is a training-dynamics problem, not an ELBO flaw (Failure Mode 1)

**Confidence:** high · **Verification vote:** 3-0 (multiple claims)

**Claim.** Posterior/KL collapse (CHART failure mode 1) is not a fundamental VAE flaw but arises from training dynamics and optimization geometry: the inference network lags the true (moving-target) posterior early in training, and collapse coincides with local maxima of the log-marginal likelihood — the ELBO itself introduces no extra spurious maxima (proven for linear/pPCA VAEs). A powerful (autoregressive) decoder makes q(z|x)=p(z) and drives KL to zero.

**Mechanism / evidence.** He 2019: 'during the initial stages of training the inference network fails to approximate the model's true posterior, which is a moving target.' Lucas 2019: 'the ELBO objective for the linear VAE does not introduce additional spurious local maxima relative to log marginal likelihood' and 'posterior collapse may occur in pPCA due to local maxima in the log marginal likelihood.' Bowman 2015: 'most training runs... consistently set q(z|x) equal to the prior p(z), bringing the KL divergence term... to zero.' These are complementary causes, not mutually exclusive. Maps to CHART: kl_phase->0.003 under beta=1 is the textbook powerful-decoder shortcut through audio features h.

- https://arxiv.org/abs/1901.05534
- https://arxiv.org/pdf/1911.02469

### 2. Three established fix-families for continuous-latent collapse (Failure Mode 1)

**Confidence:** high · **Verification vote:** 3-0 (multiple claims)

**Claim.** Established fixes for continuous-latent collapse fall into three families: (a) minimum-rate constraints that guarantee non-zero KL WITHOUT weakening the decoder or modifying the ELBO (delta-VAE, free-bits); (b) decoder weakening (word/feature dropout forcing reliance on z); (c) training-schedule/optimization fixes (KL annealing, and aggressive inference-network optimization guided by mutual information). CHART has ad-hoc applied free-bits and a latent-only decoder; it has NOT tried delta-VAE's posterior-family constraint or He-2019 aggressive encoder updates.

**Mechanism / evidence.** delta-VAE (Razavi 2019): 'constraining the variational family for the posterior to have a minimum distance to the prior' while using 'the most powerful generative models as decoders' — avoids the design tradeoff that 'either weakens the capacity of the decoder or requires augmenting the objective.' He 2019 fix: 'aggressively optimize the inference network before each model update, guided by... mutual information' — no architectural change. Bowman 2015 word dropout: 'randomly replace some fraction of the conditioned-on word tokens with... unk. This forces the model to rely on the latent variable z.' KL annealing: weight set to 0 at start, 'gradually increase this weight.' CHART's latent-only decoder is the decoder-weakening analogue; free-bits is the minimum-rate analogue; untried: delta-VAE posterior constraint, aggressive encoder updates, MI-monitored annealing.

**Sources.**
- https://arxiv.org/abs/1901.03416
- https://arxiv.org/abs/1901.05534
- https://arxiv.org/pdf/1511.06349


### 3. CHART is a Dynamical VAE (DVAE) — the comparative map (Girin 2021)

**Confidence:** high · **Verification vote:** 3-0 (two claims)

**Claim.** CHART belongs to the Dynamical VAE (DVAE) family; the canonical comparative reference (Girin 2021) unifies seven instances (DKF, KVAE, STORN, VRNN, SRNN, RVAE, DSAE) under common notation, providing the map for how priors/posteriors are structured in sequential SSM-VAEs CHART must situate against.

**Mechanism / evidence.** Girin 2021: 'a general class of models called Dynamical Variational Autoencoders (DVAEs) that encompasses a large subset of these temporal VAE extensions' and 'seven different instances of DVAE... homogenize the notations.' CHART's bar-pointer latent z_t with learned transition prior + emission decoder is squarely a DVAE. Note: DVBF is named in CHART context but is not one of the seven; DKF/KVAE cover the Kalman-VAE-style slot.

### 4. The decisive lever for the free-run gap: an observation-conditioned prior (VRNN) (Failure Mode 2)

**Confidence:** high · **Verification vote:** 3-0 (two claims)

**Claim.** The key sequential-VAE design lever for CHART's free-running gap is an OBSERVATION-HISTORY-CONDITIONED prior. VRNN makes p(z_t) depend on all preceding inputs via the RNN hidden state h_{t-1}, and its ablation VRNN-I (time-independent prior, equivalent to STORN's fixed N(0,I)) performs measurably worse. CHART's prior being 'audio-blind' at inference is precisely the VRNN-I regime that is known to underperform.

**Mechanism / evidence.** VRNN (Chung 2015): 'makes the prior distribution of the latent random variable at timestep t dependent on all the preceding inputs via the RNN hidden state ht-1... expected to improve the representational power.' Ablation: 'If the variant... (VRNN-I) does not apply the operation (a), then the prior becomes independent across timesteps' and performs worse on speech/handwriting LL. Direct CHART implication: making the transition prior condition on the frontend activations h (which CHART's ad-hoc audio-conditioned prior-mean correction approximates) is the literature-backed fix, not a hack.

**Sources.**
- https://arxiv.org/pdf/1506.02216


### 5. Enforce the latent to obey its learned transition (DVBF / SRNN) (Failure Mode 2)

**Confidence:** high · **Verification vote:** 3-0 (multiple claims)

**Claim.** Structuring latent dynamics as an identified Markovian state-space model — forcing the latent to obey the learned transition (DVBF infers transition parameters/noise, not the state directly; SRNN parameterizes SSM transitions by an NN on a deterministic RNN hidden state) — enforces state-space assumptions, improves latent information content, and enables realistic long-term free-running prediction. This is the architectural justification for CHART free-running its prior at inference.

**Mechanism / evidence.** DVBF (Karl 2017): 'enabling backpropagation through transitions enforces state space assumptions and significantly improves information content of the latent embedding. This also enables realistic long-term prediction.' SRNN (Fraccaro 2016): 'parameterizing the state transitions of the SSM with a neural network that depends on the hidden state of a recurrent neural network,' separating deterministic and stochastic layers. Caveat: DVBF evidence is on physics (pendulum/balls), not low-SNR music — domain transfer untested. CHART should consider DVBF-style inference (infer the stochastic input/transition params rather than z directly) to force its bar-pointer dynamics to be free-run-faithful.

**Sources.**
- https://openreview.net/forum?id=HyTqHL5xg
- https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper/2016/file/208e43f0e45c4c78cafadb83d2888cb6-Reviews.html


### 6. Z-Forcing: an auxiliary prediction cost that beats KL annealing (Failure Modes 1+2)

**Confidence:** high · **Verification vote:** 3-0 (multiple claims)

**Claim.** An auxiliary backward-prediction cost (Z-Forcing) keeps sequential latents alive and OUTPERFORMS KL annealing; its inference structure (training-only backward RNN, forward-only free-run at generation) is exactly CHART's train-teacher-forced vs free-run-prior mismatch. CHART has NOT tried an auxiliary cost forcing latents to predict future/backward context.

**Mechanism / evidence.** Z-Forcing (Goyal 2017): 'an auxiliary cost which forces them to reconstruct the state of the backward recurrent network. This provides the latent variables with a task-independent objective,' and 'We found this strategy to perform better than alternative approaches such as KL annealing.' Inference: 'the approximate posterior is augmented with a RNN that runs backward through the sequence' (training-only). CHART analogue: add an auxiliary loss making z_t predict future onset activations / backward state — a concrete untried fix for both collapse and the free-run gap.

**Sources.**
- https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2017/file/900c563bfd2c48c16701acca83ad858a-Paper.pdf


### 7. The train→inference drift is canonical exposure bias; scheduled sampling is itself biased (Failure Mode 2)

**Confidence:** high · **Verification vote:** 3-0

**Claim.** CHART's train->inference free-running drift (failure mode 2) is the canonical exposure-bias problem. Both maximum-likelihood teacher forcing AND scheduled sampling (CHART's ad-hoc patch) suffer exposure bias and a train/inference metric mismatch — so scheduled sampling is not a clean fix and is itself known to be biased.

**Mechanism / evidence.** ACL Findings 2024 (2406.09393): 'the standard training methods, including maximum likelihood (teacher forcing) and scheduled sampling, suffer from exposure bias and a mismatch between metrics employed during training and inference.' Caveat: the magnitude/severity of exposure bias is debated (He 2019, 'Quantifying Exposure Bias') — existence is uncontested, practical impact varies. Implication for CHART: scheduled sampling alleviates but does not eliminate the gap; an observation-conditioned prior (VRNN) + dynamics enforcement (DVBF) attack the root cause more directly.

**Sources.**
- https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.09393


### 8. Generative-vs-discriminative crossover explains the ~0.10 high-SNR deficit (Failure Mode 3)

**Confidence:** high · **Verification vote:** 3-0 (multiple claims)

**Claim.** The generative-vs-discriminative tradeoff (Ng & Jordan, extended multiclass 2023) explains CHART's observation that its structured prior rides ~0.10 below peak-picking on clean/high-SNR data: discriminative models have LOWER asymptotic error but need O(n) samples; generative/structured models converge in O(log n) and win only in the small-data / low-SNR regime, with a crossover. CHART's bar-pointer prior is therefore a regularizer whose value is at low SNR / structured-output / uncertainty, not high-SNR accuracy.

**Mechanism / evidence.** Ng & Jordan 2001: 'while discriminative learning has lower asymptotic error, a generative classifier may also approach its (higher) asymptotic error much faster'; two regimes — 'the generative model has already approached its asymptotic error and is thus doing better' (small data) vs 'the discriminative model approaches its lower asymptotic error and does better' (large data); naive Bayes needs O(log n), logistic regression O(n). Multiclass extension (2302.02334) confirms the same orders. Caveats: results derived under naive-Bayes conditional independence; follow-up (Xue & Titterington 2008) found mixed empirical support — the crossover is a strong heuristic, not a universal law. Direct CHART mapping: structured/DBN post-processing helps a weak frontend at low SNR and hurts a strong frontend (Beat This) on clean data — exactly the predicted crossover.

**Sources.**
- https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper/2001/file/7b7a53e239400a13bd6be6c91c4f6c4e-Paper.pdf
- https://arxiv.org/pdf/2302.02334

(7) BN-VAE, cyclical KL annealing, skip-VAE, professor forcing, and madmom-DBN-vs-Beat-This specifics were mentioned in the brief but did not produce surviving verified claims — absence of evidence here, not evidence of absence.
